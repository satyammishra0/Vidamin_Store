How Many Calories Do You Need? (Simple Formula for Indians)

Before choosing any meal plan, you must calculate your energy requirements. Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a 24-hour cycle. To find your baseline maintenance calories, multiply your total bodyweight in kilograms by 30 to 32, depending on your daily activity levels.

Once you have your maintenance baseline, you must choose your target path:

  • For muscle hypertrophy (lean bulking): Add a moderate surplus of 300 to 500 calories to your baseline daily.
  • For body fat reduction (cutting): Subtract 400 to 500 calories from your baseline to force your system to utilize stored adipose tissue.

Your daily macronutrient distribution must prioritize protein to repair muscle fibers. Aim for 1.6 to 2.0 grams of high-quality protein per kilogram of body weight. Fill the remaining caloric limits with complex carbohydrates for training fuel and clean fats for hormonal health.

Example for 70kg Individual:

  • Maintenance: ~2,100 kcal
  • Lean Bulking Target: ~2,500 kcal
  • Daily Protein Target: 112g - 140g

7-Day Indian Gym Diet Plan — Muscle Building (Bulking)

To build quality lean muscle mass, your body requires a consistent energy surplus paired with nutrient-dense fuel. This 7-day Indian meal plan utilizes clean local ingredients that are easily available in Shuklaganj and Unnao markets.

Upon Waking (6:00 AM) 1 glass of warm water, 5-6 soaked almonds, and 3-4 walnuts.
Breakfast (8:30 AM) 70g rolled oats cooked in 250ml whole milk, topped with 1 sliced banana, 1 tablespoon of raw organic honey, and 1 scoop of authentic whey protein isolate (from our Vidamin Store). Alternatively, 4 whole boiled eggs and 2 slices of whole-wheat toasted bread.
Mid-Morning (11:00 AM) 150g sweet potato or boiled potato with black salt and freshly squeezed lemon juice, paired with 100g low-fat paneer or 150g fresh curd.
Lunch (1:30 PM) 150g clean basmati rice or 3 medium whole-wheat rotis, 150g grilled chicken breast (cooked with minimal olive or mustard oil), 1 cup of thick yellow dal, and a large plate of mixed green salad (cucumber, beetroot, onion). For vegetarians, replace chicken breast with 150g pan-seared tofu or high-protein paneer.
Pre-Workout (5:00 PM) 2 slices of multi-grain bread spread with 2 tablespoons of single-ingredient Nut Cut Peanut Butter, 1 large banana, and a strong cup of unsweetened black coffee.
Post-Workout (7:30 PM) 1 scoop of premium whey protein mixed in 300ml cold water, taken immediately at the gym, paired with 80g white rice or 1 ripe banana to replenish depleted muscle glycogen stores.
Dinner (9:30 PM) 120g grilled fish or pan-seared chicken breast, 2 warm whole-wheat rotis, 1 cup of mixed boiled seasonal vegetables (broccoli, carrots, beans), and 1 cup of curd. For vegetarians, replace fish with a thick bowl of soya chunks bhurji (100g soy chunks).
Before Bed (10:30 PM) 1 glass (200ml) of warm milk with half a teaspoon of organic turmeric powder to promote tissue repair and deep sleep.

7-Day Indian Gym Diet Plan — Fat Loss (Cutting)

Fat loss is purely a function of maintaining a controlled caloric deficit while keeping protein high to preserve metabolically active muscle tissue. This meal plan cuts out heavy liquid dairy, high glycemic indices, and unnecessary cooking fats.

Upon Waking (6:00 AM) 1 cup of unsweetened green tea or warm lemon water, taken before morning cardio.
Breakfast (8:30 AM) Egg white omelette made from 5 egg whites, cooked with chopped spinach, onions, and tomatoes, using zero oil. Paired with 1 slice of dry whole-wheat toast. For vegetarians, 1 bowl of high-protein besan chilla (75g chickpea flour) with 100g grated low-fat paneer.
Mid-Morning (11:00 AM) 150g fresh papaya or 1 green apple, paired with 10-12 unsalted roasted peanuts or 4-5 almonds.
Lunch (1:30 PM) 80g basmati rice or 2 small whole-wheat rotis, 150g boiled or steamed chicken breast cooked with minimal spices, 1 bowl of watery mung dal, and a double serving of green leafy salad to maximize fullness.
Pre-Workout (5:00 PM) 1 cup of strong black coffee and 1 apple or 3 egg whites. Do not consume heavy carbohydrate meals before training while cutting.
Post-Workout (7:30 PM) 1 scoop of authentic whey protein isolate in cold water. Avoid high-sugar fruits or heavy carbohydrates at this time if you are in a deep cutting phase.
Dinner (9:30 PM) 150g grilled paneer (for vegetarians) or 150g steamed fish, paired with a massive bowl of steamed vegetables (cauliflower, cabbage, beans) and a warm bowl of tomato soup.
Before Bed (10:30 PM) 1 cup of warm water with 1 capsule of organic Ashwagandha to lower physiological cortisol levels and improve recovery.

Push Pull Legs Split — Best Workout Plan for Indian Gym-Goers

The Push-Pull-Legs (PPL) split is the most efficient, biomechanically logical training methodology for natural lifters. It groups muscles that work in unison, allowing you to train each muscle group twice every calendar week with optimal rest periods between heavy sessions.

Monday & Thursday — Push Days

Target the chest, shoulders, and triceps. Focus on flat barbell bench press (4 sets of 6-8 reps), overhead barbell press (3 sets of 8-10 reps), incline dumbbell press (3 sets of 10-12 reps), lateral dumbbell raises (4 sets of 15 reps), and overhead tricep dumbbell extensions (3 sets of 12 reps).

Tuesday & Friday — Pull Days

Target the back, biceps, and rear delts. Focus on weighted pullups or lat pulldowns (4 sets of 8-10 reps), barbell bent-over rows (3 sets of 6-8 reps), seated cable rows (3 sets of 10-12 reps), face pulls (4 sets of 15 reps), and heavy barbell bicep curls (3 sets of 8-10 reps).

Wednesday & Saturday — Leg Days

Target the quads, hamstrings, calves, and lower back. Focus on barbell back squats (4 sets of 6-8 reps), Romanian deadlifts (3 sets of 8-10 reps), leg press (3 sets of 10-12 reps), leg curls (3 sets of 15 reps), and standing calf raises (4 sets of 20 reps).

Sunday — Active Recovery: Perform 45 minutes of low-intensity steady-state cardio, mobility stretches, or rest completely.

Common Mistakes Beginners Make at the Gym

I have spent more than 10 years coaching in the trenches, and I see beginners repeat the same critical errors daily. Avoid these five mistakes to save years of wasted effort:

1. Ego Lifting Loading more weight onto a barbell than you can handle leads to terrible execution form, zero muscle stimulation, and high joint injury rates. Focus on the mind-muscle connection.
2. Neglecting Diet Consistency You cannot out-train a poor diet. Going hard in the weight room but failing to hit your daily protein and caloric targets results in zero muscle growth.
3. Inconsistent Sleep and Recovery Muscle tissue does not grow while you are training; it grows when you sleep. Failing to get 7 to 8 hours of deep night sleep severely limits muscle hypertrophy and strength gains.
4. Ignoring Progressive Overload If you lift the same 10kg dumbbell for 12 reps every week for six months, your body has no biological reason to grow. You must systematically increase weight, reps, or set volume.
5. Over-relying on Supplements Supplements are designed to add a 5% edge to a solid foundation. Buying expensive pre-workouts and fat-burners before fixing your daily meals is a complete waste of money.
